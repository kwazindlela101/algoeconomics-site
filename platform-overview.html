03 ¬∑ Methodology

How the Intelligence Is Built

This is the first system that treats capital movement as a full-cycle engineering problem, not a speculative judgment call. Every score is derived, not assumed.

01

Governance Signal Capture ‚Äî ACI RF Framework

Ten risk factors across three pillars ‚Äî Institutional Integrity, Sector Exposure, and Execution Risk ‚Äî are scored and weighted to produce the ACI score. Each factor is independently calibrated per market and sector. This is not a perception index. It is a structural governance measurement.

02

Exit Feasibility Quantification ‚Äî ERS Framework

Five components ‚Äî Exit Route Analysis (35%), Capital Repatriation (25%), Market Liquidity Depth (25%), IRR Benchmarking (15%), and Exit Timeline Probability (5%) ‚Äî are scored and weighted to produce the Exit Readiness Score. Each component draws on IFC, CDC, AfDB, IMF, and UNCTAD data.

03

Live Regulatory Intelligence Layer

Active forced divestment mandates, capital repatriation controls, ownership transfer requirements, and sector re-nationalisation precedents are monitored as live signals across all covered markets. Each alert feeds directly into ERS component scoring. This transforms static scores into live intelligence.

04

Strategic Advisory Output

The combined ACI + ERS framework produces a market-specific Strategic Advisory layer ‚Äî investment thesis, structuring requirements, exit architecture recommendations, and comparable market precedents. This is the output that goes directly into investment committee materials.
